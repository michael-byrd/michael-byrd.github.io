What is a group anyway?

A group is a set \(G\) equipped with an operation \( \star \) that satisifies four axioms.

  • A group is closed under the operation \(\star\). That is, for all \(a, b \in G\), we have \(a \star b \in G \).
  • A group is associative. That is, for all \(a,b,c \in G\), we have \(a \star (b \star c) = (a \star b) \star c\).
  • A group has an identity, \( e \in G \) such that \( e \star a = a \star e = a, \) for all \(a \in G.\)
  • Every element in the group has an inverse. That is, for all \(a \in G\), there exists an element, say \(b\) such that \( a \star b = b \star a = e \).

Some examples of groups include: the set of integers, rotations of a regular polygon, and the numbers on a clock.
